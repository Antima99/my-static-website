The History of Bingo

Bingo originated in 16th century Italy as "Il Gioco del Lotto dâ€™Italia," a lottery game played by the elite. By the 18th century, it had reached France, where it was called "Le Lotto" and became a favorite among the aristocracy. Its popularity spread across Europe before reaching the United States in the 1920s.

In America, Edwin S. Lowe, a toy salesman, modernized the game and renamed it "Bingo." He collaborated with mathematician Carl Leffler to create thousands of unique Bingo cards. The game quickly became a sensation, played in churches, community centers, and even military camps during World War II.

Today, Bingo continues to evolve. From traditional halls to online platforms, the game remains a symbol of fun and camaraderie for people across the globe.
